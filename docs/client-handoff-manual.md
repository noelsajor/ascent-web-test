## 🔒 Accessing Your CMS (Content Management System)

Instead of a clunky backend like WordPress, your website uses a highly secure, enterprise-grade system called **Sanity Studio**.

1. Navigate to your Studio URL (e.g., `https://studio.yourwebsite.com`).
2. Log in using your authorized Google Account or Email credentials. 
3. *Note: If a team member needs access to publish articles, please request that we add their email to the authorization list. Do not share credentials.*

---

## 📝 How to Publish a Blog Post

Publishing content is simple and will not break your website's layout.

1. Once logged into the Studio, look at the left-hand navigation menu.
2. Click on **"Posts"** (or Articles).
3. Click the blue **"Create new"** button (or the `+` icon) at the top.
4. Fill out the required fields:
   - **Title**: The headline of your post.
   - **Slug**: Click the "Generate" button. This creates the URL for your post (e.g., `/blog/your-new-post`).
   - **Author & Category**: Select from the dropdowns.
   - **Main Image**: Upload your cover photo.
   - **Body**: This is a rich-text editor similar to Google Docs. You can highlight text to make it bold, add links, or insert images.
5. When finished, hit **"Publish"** in the bottom right corner. 

**Wait 2-3 Minutes!** 
Because your site is highly optimized for performance, clicking "Publish" triggers our servers to rebuild the site's code from scratch. Your new post will appear live on the website in about 2 to 3 minutes.

## 🖼️ Best Practices for Uploading Images

Large images will drastically slow down your website and hurt your Google SEO ranking. Please follow these rules:

1. **Size Matters**: Never upload an image larger than **500 KB**. 
2. **Resizing**: If your photo is straight from a camera (e.g., 5MB), use a free tool like [Squoosh.app](https://squoosh.app/) or [TinyPNG.com](https://tinypng.com/) to compress it before uploading it to the Studio.
3. **Format**: Standard `.jpg` or `.png` files are perfect. Our system will automatically convert them to next-generation formats (`.webp`) behind the scenes for speed.

### Using the Image "Hotspot" Tool
If you upload an image and cut off an important part (like someone's face) on mobile devices, use Sanity's hotspot tool!
- Click "Edit" on your uploaded image.
- Click the "Hotspot" target icon.
- Drag the circle over the most important part of the photo (like a face or product). The website will now ensure that specific point is never cropped out, regardless of what screen size the user is on.
